About the Author

Imam Abu Bakr Ibn Abi Shaybah, born Abdullah ibn Muhammad ibn al-Qadi Abi Shaybah in Kufa in 159 AH, was a towering figure in the early Islamic scholarly tradition. Known as “the master of hadith preservation” and the author of major works such as Al-Musannaf, Al-Musnad, and his Tafsir, Ibn Abi Shaybah grew up in a family devoted to the study of knowledge. His elder brother, Uthman ibn Abi Shaybah, and his nephew Abu Ja‘far Muhammad ibn Uthman, were also prominent scholars, forming a household of learning. He studied under notable teachers, including Sharik ibn Abdullah al-Qadi, and was a contemporary of luminaries like Ahmad ibn Hanbal, Ishaq ibn Rahwayh, Ali ibn al-Madini, and Yahya ibn Ma‘in.

From a young age, Ibn Abi Shaybah demonstrated extraordinary memory and diligence in acquiring knowledge. He transmitted hadiths from a vast number of teachers and disciples, and his students included many prominent muhaddithun such as Abu al-Ahwas, Sulaim ibn Sulaym, and Abd al-Salam ibn Harb, among others. He was widely recognized for his authenticity, precision, and reliability in narrations. Leading scholars praised him highly: Ahmad ibn Hanbal described him as trustworthy and deeply knowledgeable, while Ali ibn al-Madini acknowledged his exceptional memorization and understanding of hadith.

Scholarly Contributions and Works

Ibn Abi Shaybah authored numerous works across hadith, jurisprudence, and tafsir, including:

  • Al-Musannaf: His most famous work, systematically arranged by topics and subtopics of hadith, with full isnad chains and thematic organization.
  • Al-Musnad: A collection of hadiths transmitted through chains linked to specific companions.
  • Al-Tafsir: Commentary on the Quran.
  • Al-Ahkam: Fiqh rulings based on authentic narrations.
  • Other works: Kitab al-Iman, Kitab al-Adab, Kitab Thawab al-Quran, Kitab al-Jumal, Kitab al-Futuh, and books refuting theological sects such as the Mu‘tazila.

Significance of Al-Musannaf

Al-Musannaf fi al-Ahadith wa al-Athar is an encyclopedic reference covering the sayings of the Prophet ﷺ, reports from the companions, and the opinions of early scholars. It preserves the authentic traditions while also highlighting lesser-known narrations that provide insight into the social, ethical, and legal practices of early Islam. Compiled with meticulous attention to chains of narration and verified against multiple sources, it is widely cited by later scholars such as Ibn Hibban, Al-Bukhari, and Muslim ibn al-Hajjaj.

Translation Notes

The English translation has been reviewed and supervised by Sheikh Abu Sultana, ensuring clarity, accuracy, and faithfulness to the original text. Both Arabic and English are presented together to allow readers to verify isnads, study narrators, and understand the meanings in context. Special care has been taken to reconcile differences found in old editions and manuscripts, correct errors, and standardize numbering for easier reference.

Purpose of This Online Edition

This online version aims to make Musannaf Ibn Abi Shaybah widely accessible. Users can search by hadith number, navigate by volume and chapter, and read individual hadiths with both Arabic and English texts. This platform preserves the scholarly legacy of Ibn Abi Shaybah, facilitates study, and promotes deeper understanding of early Islamic knowledge and practice.

May Allah grant benefit from this work and enable readers to implement and cherish the guidance of the Prophet ﷺ and his companions.

Universal Significance

Even for those who are not religious, Al-Musannaf fi al-Ahadith wa al-Athar represents a remarkable human achievement. Compiled over nine centuries ago, its meticulous organization, linguistic richness, and sheer volume are extraordinary—many modern languages and literary traditions did not even exist at the time. Very few scholars, such as Imam Abu Bakr Ibn Abi Shaybah, Rahimahullah, produced works of such scale and precision. The text offers profound insights into language, culture, law, and society of the early Islamic world, making it an invaluable resource not only for religious studies but also for historians, linguists, and anyone interested in the intellectual heritage of humanity.
